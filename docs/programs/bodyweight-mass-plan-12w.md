# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Bodyweight Mass Plan (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Home Training  
Primary Goal: Maximum Muscle Gain with Bodyweight  
Training Style: High Volume + Near Failure  
Frequency: 5-6 Days / Week  
Equipment: None  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week bodyweight hypertrophy plan built around high-volume loading.
- Uses split structure for chest/triceps, legs, and core development.
- Includes near-failure strategy and advanced paused-rep progression.
- Designed to maximize muscle stimulus while preserving movement quality.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Mass Foundation)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Chest + Triceps circuit.
2. Day 2 and Day 5: Legs circuit.
3. Day 3: Core circuit.
4. Day 6: Walk 20 min.
5. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Expansion)
1. Week 3 increases all primary training days to 4 rounds.
2. Week 4 scales reps and hold durations across all sessions.
3. Workload rises while rest control and exercise quality stay strict.

### Weeks 5-6 (Failure Focus)
1. Rule: Last set of key movements is performed to technical failure.
2. Week 5 keeps 4-round structure with failure strategy.
3. Week 6 increases to 5 rounds.
4. Technical quality remains mandatory at end of sets.

### Weeks 7-8 (High Volume Block)
1. Higher reps and hold times across chest, legs, and core sessions.
2. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps
   - Squats max reps
   - Repeat 3 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (Advanced Sets)
1. Push-ups include 2-sec bottom hold.
2. Squats include 2-sec bottom pause.
3. Week 9 runs 5 rounds; Week 10 progresses to 6 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Volume)
1. Week 11 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps
   - Lunges 20/leg
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 reaches highest rep and hold targets at 6 rounds.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Chase technical failure, not sloppy failure.
- Keep rep tempo stable to maximize muscular tension.
- Track quality reps per round to measure progress.
- Use rest day discipline to support high-volume recovery.

## Safety Protocol
- Stop before bad form appears.
- Quality over ego in every session.
- Reduce rounds when movement control drops.
