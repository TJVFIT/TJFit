# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Calisthenics Growth System (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Home Training  
Primary Goal: Muscle Growth + Calisthenics Control  
Training Style: Strength + Skill Progression  
Frequency: 5-6 Days / Week  
Equipment: None (floor, wall, chair variations allowed)  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week system combining muscle-building volume and calisthenics skill progression.
- Built around push mechanics, lower-body control, balance, and trunk stability.
- Introduces decline push-ups, handstand holds, and assisted pistol patterns over time.
- Designed to grow strength and motor control together without sacrificing form quality.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Foundation Block)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Push focus (push-up progressions + plank).
2. Day 2 and Day 5: Legs + balance (squats, lunges, single-leg stand, wall sit).
3. Day 3: Core + control (sit-ups, leg raises, plank).
4. Day 6: Walk 20 min.
5. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Build)
1. Week 3 increases all primary sessions to 4 rounds.
2. Week 4 raises reps and hold durations across all sessions.
3. Maintain strict technique before increasing speed.

### Weeks 5-6 (Skill Intro)
1. Day 1 adds decline push-ups and wall handstand holds.
2. Day 2 adds assisted pistol-squat progression and lunge hold work.
3. Week 6 increases total rounds to 5 while preserving control quality.

### Weeks 7-8 (Skill Growth)
1. Higher reps and longer handstand hold durations.
2. Lower-body skill work progresses with higher assisted pistol volume.
3. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Handstand hold max
   - Push-ups max
   - Repeat 2 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (Advanced Control)
1. Decline push-ups and handstand become central strength-skill drivers.
2. Lower-body volume continues to rise with controlled pistol progression.
3. Week 10 increases to 6 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Block)
1. Week 11 finisher:
   - Handstand hold max
   - Pistol squat 10/leg
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 reaches peak controlled volume at 6 rounds with top skill targets.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Treat skill drills as precision work, not fatigue chasing.
- Keep scapular control and core bracing active in all push and handstand patterns.
- Progress skill variations only when rep mechanics remain stable.
- Track time-under-tension and clean reps for objective progression.

## Safety Protocol
- Control is always greater than ego.
- Do not rush skill progressions.
- Stop any set if technical quality falls.
