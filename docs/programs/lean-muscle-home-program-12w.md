# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Lean Muscle Home Program (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Home Training  
Primary Goal: Lean, Aesthetic Muscle Development  
Training Style: Moderate Reps + Volume + Symmetry  
Frequency: 5-6 Days / Week  
Equipment: None  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week bodyweight program built for lean muscle shape and balanced symmetry.
- Combines moderate-volume strength work with aesthetic detail progressions.
- Uses supersets, tempo control, and timed phases to improve definition.
- Designed to create a polished physique without sacrificing movement quality.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Shape Foundation)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Upper body shape circuit.
2. Day 2 and Day 5: Lower body shape circuit.
3. Day 3: Core and tightening circuit.
4. Day 6: Walk 20 min.
5. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Build)
1. Week 3 increases primary training days to 4 rounds.
2. Week 4 scales reps and hold durations in all blocks.
3. Emphasis remains balanced output and clean execution.

### Weeks 5-6 (Aesthetic Volume)
1. Upper-body detail work adds slow push-ups and shoulder taps.
2. Lower-body detail work adds pulse squats and lunge hold positions.
3. Week 6 increases total rounds to 5.

### Weeks 7-8 (Lean Growth)
1. Rep targets increase across upper, lower, and core.
2. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps
   - Squats max reps
   - Repeat 2 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (Detail Phase)
1. Timed structure begins:
   - 40 sec work / 20 sec rest
2. Applied to upper, lower, and core day formats.
3. Week 10 increases to 7 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Aesthetic)
1. Week 11 adds finisher:
   - Jump Squats x 12
   - Push-ups x 12
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 shifts to:
   - 45 sec work / 15 sec rest
   - 7 rounds
3. Day 6 conditioning rises to 40-minute walk.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Keep bilateral symmetry in mind on every unilateral movement.
- Maintain controlled rep speed to maximize visual muscle detail.
- Prioritize posture and core engagement on all timed blocks.
- Track weekly quality completion rather than chasing rushed reps.

## Safety Protocol
- Focus on form and control first.
- No rushing reps in any phase.
- Stop any set when technique degrades.
