# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Bodyweight Shred System (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Home Training  
Primary Goal: Fat Loss + Muscle Tone  
Training Style: Strength + Cardio Mix  
Frequency: 5-6 Days / Week  
Equipment: None  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week bodyweight system designed to reduce fat and sharpen muscle tone.
- Combines strength circuits, cardio blocks, supersets, and HIIT timing.
- Progression is structured by reps, rounds, rest reduction, and interval upgrades.
- Built for clients moving from beginner baseline to advanced work density.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Base Build)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Full-body strength circuits (squats, push-ups, lunges, plank).
2. Day 2 and Day 5: Cardio circuit (jumping jacks, high knees, mountain climbers).
3. Day 3: Walk 20-25 min.
4. Day 6: Walk 25-30 min.
5. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Increase)
1. Week 3: Round count rises on strength and cardio days.
2. Week 4: Reps and hold times increase; rest reduced to 25-30 sec.
3. Day 6 adds light jog + walk blend for conditioning progression.

### Weeks 5-6 (Superset Phase)
1. Superset pair 1: Squats -> Jump Squats.
2. Superset pair 2: Push-ups -> Shoulder Taps.
3. Superset pair 3: Lunges -> Squat Hold.
4. Week 6 increases total rounds and cardio density.

### Weeks 7-8 (Intensity Rise)
1. Main strength movements scale in reps and hold duration.
2. Cardio intervals increase with 6-round structure.
3. Week 8 adds daily finisher:
   - Burpees x 10
   - Plank 30 sec
   - Repeat 3 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (HIIT Structure)
1. Timed model: 40 sec work / 20 sec rest.
2. Strength and cardio days both follow interval structure.
3. Week 10 increases to 7 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Phase)
1. Week 11 adds finisher:
   - Jump Squats x 12
   - Burpees x 12
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 model:
   - 45 sec work / 15 sec rest
   - 7 rounds
3. Day 3 walk increases to 45 min.
4. Day 6 jog increases to 25 min.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Keep every rep clean, stable, and controlled before adding speed.
- Use strict breathing control during transitions to manage output.
- Prioritize posture and core tension in all timed intervals.
- Progress only when current volume is completed with quality.

## Safety Protocol
- Stop instantly if pain, dizziness, or movement instability appears.
- Reduce rounds when form quality declines across consecutive sessions.
- Stay hydrated and maintain recovery discipline.
