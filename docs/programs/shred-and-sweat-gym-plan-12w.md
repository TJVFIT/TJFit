# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Shred & Sweat Gym Plan (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Gym Training  
Primary Goal: Fast Fat Loss + Conditioning  
Training Style: Supersets + Circuits + Cardio  
Frequency: 5-6 Days / Week  
Equipment: Full Gym  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week gym shredding protocol built for aggressive fat-loss momentum.
- Combines high-density resistance circuits with cardio and HIIT progression.
- Uses superset blocks to elevate calorie demand while protecting muscle output.
- Structured to improve conditioning and body composition week by week.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Circuit Foundation)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Upper-body circuit plus treadmill cardio.
2. Day 2 and Day 5: Lower-body circuit plus bike cardio.
3. Day 3 and Day 6: Cardio-focused support sessions.
4. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Density Build)
1. Week 3 scales upper and lower circuits to 4 rounds.
2. Week 4 reduces rest to 30-40 sec to increase training density.
3. Cardio baseline reaches 20 min.

### Weeks 5-6 (Superset Phase)
1. Upper supersets pair compound lifts with bodyweight/isolation finish moves.
2. Lower supersets pair machine strength work with functional bodyweight drills.
3. Week 6 progresses to 5 rounds and 25-min cardio support.

### Weeks 7-8 (High Density)
1. Reps rise while rest windows tighten to 20-30 sec.
2. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Burpees x 10
   - Jump Squats x 10
   - Repeat 3 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (HIIT Shift)
1. Lifting remains in structured 4-round muscle-retention format.
2. Cardio transitions to HIIT:
   - 30 sec sprint
   - 30 sec walk
3. Week 9 uses 12 rounds; Week 10 uses 14 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Sweat)
1. Week 11 finisher:
   - Push-ups x 15
   - Jump Squats x 12
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 raises key lifting sessions to 5 rounds.
3. HIIT reaches 15 rounds at 30/30.
4. Additional long-cardio and light-cardio days complete peak week.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Keep circuit transitions efficient but never uncontrolled.
- Prioritize technical quality when rest windows are short.
- Treat HIIT rounds as repeatable output, not one-round maximal effort.
- Track weekly density markers: rounds completed, rest discipline, and form quality.

## Safety Protocol
- Short rest increases fatigue, so keep form clean.
- Stop immediately if technique degrades under fatigue.
- Reduce intervals or rounds when recovery quality drops.
