# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## 12-Week Home Strength Gain

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Home Training  
Primary Goal: Strength Development (Not Just Size)  
Training Style: Slow Tempo + Pauses + Control  
Frequency: 5 Days / Week  
Equipment: None  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week bodyweight strength system focused on controlled mechanical tension.
- Uses tempo reps, pause holds, and progressive rounds to increase true strength.
- Split structure covers upper, lower, and core strength development.
- Designed for strict execution quality over speed or ego-driven volume.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Strength Foundation)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Upper strength with tempo push patterns.
2. Day 2 and Day 5: Lower strength with controlled squats and lunge mechanics.
3. Day 3: Core strength under slow and stable execution.
4. Day 6: Walk 20 min.
5. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Build)
1. Week 3 increases primary sessions to 4 rounds.
2. Week 4 scales reps and hold durations across all major sessions.
3. Tempo quality remains fixed while workload rises.

### Weeks 5-6 (Pause Phase)
1. Rule:
   - Hold bottom position for 2 sec on key lifts.
2. Week 5 applies pause strategy at 4 rounds.
3. Week 6 progresses to 5 rounds while keeping strict pauses.

### Weeks 7-8 (Strength Increase)
1. Higher rep and hold targets across upper, lower, and core.
2. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps (slow)
   - Squats max reps (slow)
   - Repeat 2 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (Advanced Control)
1. Push-ups and squats move to 4-sec eccentric plus pause.
2. Week 9 uses 5 rounds; Week 10 progresses to 6 rounds.
3. Focus remains strict control under fatigue.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Strength)
1. Week 11 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps (slow)
   - Lunges 20/leg
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 reaches peak controlled volume at 6 rounds.
3. Hold targets rise to plank and wall sit 60 sec.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Respect tempo and pause prescriptions to maximize strength transfer.
- Keep spinal alignment neutral and core braced on every set.
- Progress only when current targets are completed with clean mechanics.
- Track technical rep quality as the key KPI.

## Safety Protocol
- Slow reps increase joint stress; stop if joints hurt.
- Do not sacrifice form for additional reps.
- Scale volume if control quality drops.
