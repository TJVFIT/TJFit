# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## High Intensity Fat Burn (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Gym Training  
Primary Goal: Maximum Fat Loss + Conditioning  
Training Style: HIIT + Circuits + Minimal Rest  
Frequency: 6 Days / Week  
Equipment: Full Gym  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week high-output fat-loss protocol for advanced conditioning demands.
- Combines full-body circuits, sprint intervals, and dense supersets.
- Progressively compresses rest while increasing HIIT round volume.
- Designed for rapid composition change with strict recovery management.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Foundation Block)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Full-body circuit plus moderate cardio.
2. Day 2 and Day 5: HIIT sprint/walk interval days.
3. Day 3: Lower-body burn circuit plus bike support.
4. Day 6: Dedicated cardio session.
5. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume and Pace Build)
1. Circuit days progress to 4 rounds.
2. HIIT rounds increase and sprint duration rises to 30 sec.
3. Rest windows tighten to improve density.

### Weeks 5-6 (High Burn Superset Phase)
1. Full-body sessions move into aggressive superset format.
2. Lower-body days combine machine and bodyweight pairings.
3. Week 6 rises to 5 rounds and higher HIIT volume.

### Weeks 7-8 (Extreme Density)
1. High-density rounds with very short rest windows.
2. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Burpees x 12
   - Jump Squats x 12
   - Repeat 3 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (Advanced HIIT)
1. Resistance sessions remain for muscle retention.
2. HIIT becomes dominant workload:
   - 30 sec sprint
   - 30 sec walk
3. Week 9 uses 20 rounds; Week 10 uses 22 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Intensity)
1. Week 11 finisher:
   - Push-ups x 15
   - Jump Squats x 15
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 peaks at 25 HIIT rounds (30/30).
3. Includes one lighter form-focused day and extended cardio support.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Keep every rep technically clean despite high heart-rate conditions.
- Use sprint intervals as repeatable quality output, not all-out chaos.
- Track density metrics weekly: total rounds, rest discipline, and form score.
- Recovery quality determines performance quality in this protocol.

## Safety Protocol
- Very intense system: stop immediately if form breaks.
- Do not force reps under fatigue collapse.
- Recovery is key to safe completion and results.
