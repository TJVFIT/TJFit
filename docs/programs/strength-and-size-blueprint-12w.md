# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Strength & Size Blueprint (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Gym Training  
Primary Goal: Strength + Muscle Size  
Training Style: Heavy Compounds + Hypertrophy Work  
Frequency: 5 Days / Week  
Equipment: Full Gym  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week hybrid system built to increase both raw strength and muscular size.
- Combines low-rep heavy compounds with strategic hypertrophy accessories.
- Progresses through strength push, heavy phase, and size-shift phases.
- Designed to create powerful performance and visible physique growth together.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Foundation Block)
1. Day 1: Chest strength and size.
2. Day 2: Back strength and size.
3. Day 3: Legs strength and size.
4. Day 4: Shoulders.
5. Day 5: Arms.
6. Day 6: Rest or light walk.
7. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Base)
1. Week 3 scales all split sessions to 4 sets.
2. Week 4 keeps 4 sets with controlled heavy-lift rest.
3. Strength and hypertrophy balance remains stable.

### Weeks 5-6 (Strength Push)
1. Main compounds move into heavier rep ranges.
2. Accessories maintain hypertrophy support.
3. Week 6 increases to 5 sets.

### Weeks 7-8 (Heavy Phase)
1. Compounds push to 4-5 rep ranges with full control.
2. Rest windows expand to support quality heavy output.
3. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps
   - Squats max reps
   - Repeat 2 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (Size Shift)
1. Rep emphasis shifts toward hypertrophy.
2. Week 9 holds 5 sets; Week 10 moves to 6 sets.
3. Volume-driven size work becomes dominant.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Strength + Size)
1. Week 11 adds drop sets on final key sets.
2. Week 12 keeps 6-set structure across the split.
3. Program closes with full recovery support days.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Treat heavy compounds as technical performance lifts, not ego lifts.
- Preserve tempo and range quality before adding load.
- Use hypertrophy work to reinforce symmetry and fullness.
- Track load progression and quality reps on all major compounds weekly.

## Safety Protocol
- Heavy weights require strict form.
- Never rush reps.
- Stop sets if technique deteriorates.
