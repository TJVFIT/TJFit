# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Aesthetic Muscle Plan (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Gym Training  
Primary Goal: Balanced, Aesthetic Physique  
Training Style: Moderate Weight + Volume + Symmetry  
Frequency: 5-6 Days / Week  
Equipment: Full Gym  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week aesthetics-focused gym plan for balanced shape and visual symmetry.
- Combines moderate-load hypertrophy work with detail-oriented supersets.
- Progresses into timed density phases for precision and conditioning.
- Built to improve physique quality while preserving technical control.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Foundation Block)
1. Day 1: Chest + shape.
2. Day 2: Back + width.
3. Day 3: Legs + shape.
4. Day 4: Shoulders + detail.
5. Day 5: Arms + finish.
6. Day 6: Optional cardio.
7. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Base)
1. Week 3 scales all sessions to 4 sets.
2. Week 4 keeps 4 sets with controlled rest.
3. Focus remains strict contractions and symmetry quality.

### Weeks 5-6 (Detail Phase)
1. Targeted supersets introduced across chest, back, legs, and shoulders.
2. Week 6 progresses to 5 sets.
3. Emphasis remains controlled mechanics under denser workload.

### Weeks 7-8 (Aesthetic Growth)
1. Volume and reps increase across the full split.
2. Rest windows tighten for added hypertrophy density.
3. Week 8 finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps
   - Lateral Raises x 20
   - Repeat 2 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (Detail Focus)
1. Timed format begins:
   - 40 sec work / 20 sec rest
2. Applied across Day 1 to Day 5 split blocks.
3. Week 10 progresses to 7 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Physique)
1. Week 11 finisher:
   - Push-ups x 15
   - Lateral Raises x 20
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 shifts to:
   - 45 sec work / 15 sec rest
   - 7 rounds
3. Day 6 cardio extends to 40 min.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Prioritize movement symmetry and full contraction quality each set.
- Keep tempo controlled to maximize visual detail outcomes.
- Use timed blocks to sharpen conditioning without sacrificing technique.
- Track form consistency and left-right balance as key performance markers.

## Safety Protocol
- Control every rep.
- Aesthetic results come from quality, not ego.
- Stop immediately if technique degrades.
