# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Gym Mass Builder (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Gym Training  
Primary Goal: Muscle Size + Strength  
Training Style: Heavy Compounds + Hypertrophy  
Frequency: 5 Days / Week  
Equipment: Full Gym  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week mass-building system centered on compound progression and hypertrophy volume.
- Uses a full split across chest, back, legs, shoulders, and arms.
- Progresses from base loading to growth phase, heavy phase, and peak bulk execution.
- Built to maximize size gains while maintaining strict technical lifting quality.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Foundation Block)
1. Day 1: Chest.
2. Day 2: Back.
3. Day 3: Legs.
4. Day 4: Shoulders.
5. Day 5: Arms.
6. Day 6: Rest or light walk.
7. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Base)
1. Week 3 increases all sessions to 4 sets.
2. Week 4 keeps 4 sets and tightens rest on key lifts.
3. Compound and accessory balance remains fixed.

### Weeks 5-6 (Growth Phase)
1. Week 5 introduces drop-set work on final key sets.
2. Split remains stable while effort quality rises.
3. Week 6 progresses to 5 sets.

### Weeks 7-8 (Heavy Phase)
1. Primary compounds shift to lower rep ranges and heavier loading.
2. Rest windows increase to support high-quality heavy work.
3. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps
   - Squats max reps
   - Repeat 2 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (Hypertrophy Focus)
1. Rep ranges shift back toward hypertrophy targets.
2. Week 10 increases sessions to 6 sets.
3. Recovery strategy becomes crucial at this volume.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Bulk)
1. Week 11 adds drop sets on final set of key movements.
2. Week 12 keeps high-volume structure at 6 sets.
3. Program finishes with strict technique and full recovery support.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Prioritize progressive overload with clean form, not load chasing.
- Keep compound lifts technically stable before adding extra volume.
- Use accessory work to complete symmetry and detail for each muscle group.
- Track weekly load progression and quality reps for each main lift.

## Safety Protocol
- Lift heavy but controlled.
- No ego lifting.
- Stop sets when form breaks under fatigue.
