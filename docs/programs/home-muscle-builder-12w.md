# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Home Muscle Builder (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Home Training  
Primary Goal: Build Muscle Using Bodyweight  
Training Style: Slow Reps + Volume + Control  
Frequency: 5 Days / Week  
Equipment: None  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week bodyweight muscle-building system with strict technique emphasis.
- Program structure uses upper/lower/core rotation with progressive rounds.
- Slow tempo and paused-rep phases increase muscle tension without equipment.
- Designed to produce lean muscle gain and stronger movement control.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Foundation Strength)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Upper body circuit (push-ups, incline push-ups, pike push-ups, plank).
2. Day 2 and Day 5: Lower body circuit (squats, lunges, glute bridge, wall sit).
3. Day 3: Core circuit (sit-ups, leg raises, plank).
4. Day 6: Walk 20 min.
5. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Base)
1. Week 3 increases all major days to 4 rounds.
2. Week 4 increases reps and hold times across upper, lower, and core sessions.
3. Rest periods remain controlled to preserve form quality.

### Weeks 5-6 (Slow Tempo Phase)
1. Tempo rule:
   - 3 sec lowering phase
   - 1 sec lifting phase
2. Week 5 applies tempo with 4 rounds.
3. Week 6 keeps tempo and progresses to 5 rounds.

### Weeks 7-8 (Volume Up)
1. Rep targets increase across all training days at 5 rounds.
2. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps
   - Squats max reps
   - Repeat 2 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (Advanced Control)
1. Push-ups include 2-sec bottom pause.
2. Squats include bottom pause.
3. Week 9 uses 5 rounds; Week 10 progresses to 6 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Phase)
1. Week 11 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups max reps
   - Lunges 20/leg
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 reaches peak targets:
   - Upper, lower, core sessions at 6 rounds
   - Plank and wall sit up to 60 sec

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Prioritize mechanical control over rep speed in every phase.
- Keep core braced during push, squat, and lunge patterns.
- Track quality reps completed, not only total reps attempted.
- Use walk and rest days to maintain consistency and recovery quality.

## Safety Protocol
- Control every rep and avoid rushing.
- Stop immediately if pain appears.
- Maintain strict form and joint alignment throughout.
