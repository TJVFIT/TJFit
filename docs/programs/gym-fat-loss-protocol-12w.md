# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Gym Fat Loss Protocol (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Gym Training  
Primary Goal: Fat Loss While Keeping Muscle  
Training Style: Weights + Cardio  
Frequency: 5-6 Days / Week  
Equipment: Full Gym  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week fat-loss system designed to preserve muscle through resistance training.
- Combines progressive gym strength blocks with structured cardio and HIIT.
- Includes superset phases for higher calorie throughput without losing lift quality.
- Built to create a leaner physique while maintaining performance capacity.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Foundation Block)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Upper body strength plus treadmill cardio.
2. Day 2 and Day 5: Lower body strength plus bike cardio.
3. Day 3 and Day 6: Cardio-focused support days.
4. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume and Density Build)
1. Week 3 increases lifting days to 4 sets.
2. Week 4 keeps 4 sets and tightens rest to improve density.
3. Cardio duration standardizes around 20 minutes.

### Weeks 5-6 (Fat Burn Superset Phase)
1. Upper day supersets combine compounds with bodyweight/isolation work.
2. Lower day supersets combine machine work with bodyweight moves.
3. Week 6 progresses to 5 sets with longer cardio support.

### Weeks 7-8 (Intensity Increase)
1. Reps increase while rest windows shorten.
2. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Burpees x 10
   - Rest 30 sec
   - Repeat 3 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (HIIT Phase)
1. Lifting remains in muscle-retention format.
2. Cardio transitions to HIIT:
   - 30 sec sprint
   - 30 sec walk
3. Week 9 uses 12 rounds; Week 10 uses 14 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Cut)
1. Week 11 adds finisher:
   - Jump Squats x 12
   - Push-ups x 12
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 raises lifting to 5 sets on key days.
3. HIIT reaches 15 rounds at 30/30 format.
4. Cardio support includes 40-min dedicated day and 30-min light day.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Keep effort high but technical quality non-negotiable.
- Preserve muscle by treating strength sessions as priority work.
- Use HIIT strategically, not recklessly, to protect recovery.
- Track load, reps, and cardio output weekly for objective progression.

## Safety Protocol
- Use correct weight and avoid ego lifting.
- Stop immediately if form collapses or pain appears.
- Reduce volume when fatigue compromises movement quality.
