# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## Sweat & Burn Blueprint (12 Weeks)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Home Training  
Primary Goal: Daily Fat Burn Habit + Steady Weight Loss  
Training Style: Short Daily Sessions (10-25 min)  
Frequency: 6-7 Days / Week  
Equipment: None  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week habit-first program built around short, repeatable daily sessions.
- Designed to improve consistency, calorie burn, and long-term adherence.
- Progression uses rounds, timed intervals, and controlled finishers.
- Focus is sustainable routine building, not extreme single-session intensity.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Habit Foundation)
1. Day 1 and Day 2: Short burn circuit (jumping jacks, squats, plank).
2. Day 3 and Day 5: Short burn variation (jumping jacks, lunges, plank).
3. Day 4 repeats Day 1.
4. Day 6: Walk 20-25 min.
5. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume Progression)
1. Week 3 increases rounds on core training days.
2. Week 4 increases interval durations and rep targets.
3. Repeat-day structure remains to reinforce daily habit behavior.

### Weeks 5-6 (Daily Upgrade)
1. Push-ups are introduced into both training templates.
2. Main training days progress to higher round counts.
3. Day 6 walking duration increases for low-impact conditioning support.

### Weeks 7-8 (Intensity Build)
1. Key movement targets increase in reps and hold times.
2. Week 8 adds daily finisher:
   - Burpees x 8
   - Rest 30 sec
   - Repeat 3 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (HIIT Style)
1. Timed model switches to 30 sec work / 20 sec rest.
2. Squat and lunge templates alternate across the week.
3. Week 10 increases to 7 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Habit)
1. Week 11 adds finisher:
   - Jump Squats x 10
   - Burpees x 10
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 main model:
   - 40 sec work / 15 sec rest
   - 7 rounds
3. Day 6 walk progresses to 40 min.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Keep sessions efficient and controlled to protect long-term consistency.
- Treat repeat days as habit anchors, not optional sessions.
- Use breathing rhythm and posture control to maintain output quality.
- Track completion rate weekly as the primary performance KPI.

## Safety Protocol
- Stop immediately if pain appears.
- Keep form clean and movement controlled.
- Stay consistent, not perfect.
