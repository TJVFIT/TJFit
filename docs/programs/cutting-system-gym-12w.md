# TJFIT PROGRAM DOSSIER
## 12-Week Cutting System (Gym)

--------------------------------------------
Program Type: Gym Training  
Primary Goal: Fat Loss + Muscle Retention + Lean Physique  
Training Style: Structured Lifting + Controlled Cardio  
Frequency: 5-6 Days / Week  
Equipment: Full Gym  
--------------------------------------------

## Executive Overview
- 12-week gym cutting protocol designed for fat loss without sacrificing muscle.
- Uses structured split training, progressive supersets, and interval cardio phases.
- Balances workload, density, and recovery for sustainable cutting performance.
- Built to drive body-fat reduction while preserving movement quality and strength.

## Weekly Training Framework

### Weeks 1-2 (Foundation Block)
1. Day 1 and Day 4: Chest-focused lifting plus treadmill cardio.
2. Day 2 and Day 5: Back-focused lifting plus bike cardio.
3. Day 3: Leg-focused lifting plus moderate walking cardio.
4. Day 6: Dedicated cardio session.
5. Day 7: Rest.

### Weeks 3-4 (Volume and Density Build)
1. Week 3 increases key lifting days to 4 sets.
2. Week 4 keeps 4 sets and reduces rest to improve training density.
3. Cardio baseline progresses to 20-minute sessions.

### Weeks 5-6 (Cut Phase Supersets)
1. Chest day and back day move into superset format.
2. Leg day combines machine work with bodyweight support drills.
3. Week 6 increases to 5 sets and longer cardio support.

### Weeks 7-8 (Hard Cut)
1. Rep targets increase and rest windows tighten.
2. Week 8 adds finisher:
   - Burpees x 10
   - Jump Squats x 10
   - Repeat 3 rounds

### Weeks 9-10 (HIIT Cut)
1. Lifting remains structured for muscle retention.
2. Cardio shifts to HIIT:
   - 30 sec sprint
   - 30 sec walk
3. Week 9 uses 12 rounds; Week 10 uses 14 rounds.

### Weeks 11-12 (Peak Cut)
1. Week 11 adds finisher:
   - Push-ups x 15
   - Jump Squats x 12
   - Repeat 3 rounds
2. Week 12 increases main sessions to 5 sets where prescribed.
3. HIIT reaches 15 rounds at 30/30.
4. Includes long-cardio and light-cardio support days.

## Coaching Notes (Premium Delivery Standard)
- Treat resistance training as muscle-retention priority work.
- Use cardio progression strategically, not aggressively all at once.
- Track load quality, rep quality, and interval consistency weekly.
- Manage fatigue proactively during low-energy cutting windows.

## Safety Protocol
- Cutting can reduce recovery and energy; avoid overtraining.
- Keep all reps strict and controlled.
- Stop sets when technique degrades.
